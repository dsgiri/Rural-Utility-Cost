# Usage Instructions

*This file provides a standardized template for outlining how a specific calculator should be used. Include this in the calculator's dedicated feature directory if the calculator involves complex steps, heavy assumptions, or risk of misuse. Skip for trivial tools.*

## Overview
**Calculator:** Fencing Materials Calculator
**Purpose:** Estimate the total number of posts, rolls of wire, and overall cost to fence a specific acreage or perimeter length.
**Target Audience:** Farmers, ranchers, and rural landowners.

## Key Rules & Assumptions
- **Rule 1:** Square acreage assumes a perfect square. Irregular property lines can increase the perimeter by 20% to 50%.
- **Rule 2:** T-posts are typically driven every 10 to 12 feet, and sturdy wooden H-braces are required at every corner and gate.
- **Rule 3:** A standard roll of barbed wire is 1,320 feet (1/4 mile).

## Step-by-Step Guide
1. **Choose Calculation Method:** Select whether you are calculating by Acres (assuming a perfectly square lot) or by explicit linear foot Perimeter.
2. **Define Fence Specifications:** Set the distance between posts (usually 10-12 feet) and the number of wire strands you intend to string (e.g., 5 strands for cattle).
3. **Add Gates:** Input the number of gates required. Every gate adds 2 robust corner/brace posts.
4. **Input Local Prices:** Add you local hardware costs for t-posts, corner posts, and wire rolls.

## Advanced Notes / Edge Cases
- **Disclaimer:** The perfect square acreage output represents the MINIMUM possible perimeter. Most real world parcels will require roughly 15-30% more materials than a perfect square. Always buy extra wire and posts.
