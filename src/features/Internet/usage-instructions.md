# Usage Instructions

*This file provides a standardized template for outlining how a specific calculator should be used. Include this in the calculator's dedicated feature directory if the calculator involves complex steps, heavy assumptions, or risk of misuse. Skip for trivial tools.*

## Overview
**Calculator:** Rural Internet Cost & Comparison
**Purpose:** Compare Starlink (Satellite) against 4G/5G LTE hotspots and fixed wireless providers over a multi-year period.
**Target Audience:** Rural homeowners, remote workers, and off-grid property owners determining the cheapest long-term broadband solution.

## Key Rules & Assumptions
- **Rule 1:** Upfront hardware costs dominate the first year of satellite internet (Starlink Dish), while cellular hotspots are typically cheaper initially.
- **Rule 2:** Monthly service limits and throttling are the primary drawbacks of LTE plans compared to unmetered Starlink data.
- **Rule 3:** Long-term Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) reveals the point at which higher monthly fees offset cheap hardware.

## Step-by-Step Guide
1. **Enter Provider Details:** Input the upfront equipment fee, installation costs, and recurring monthly fees for each provider (Starlink, LTE, or Fixed Wireless).
2. **Review TCO Chart:** Compare the cumulative costs at Year 1, Year 3, and Year 5.
3. **Analyze Data Caps:** Use the qualitative notes to rate local cell coverage versus satellite obstruction risk (trees).

## Advanced Notes / Edge Cases
- **Disclaimer:** Cost is only half the equation. Cellular internet relies heavily on proximity to the nearest tower. Starlink requires a wide, 100-degree unobstructed view of the northern sky.
