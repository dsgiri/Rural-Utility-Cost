# Usage Instructions

*This file provides a standardized template for outlining how a specific calculator should be used. Include this in the calculator's dedicated feature directory if the calculator involves complex steps, heavy assumptions, or risk of misuse. Skip for trivial tools.*

## Overview
**Calculator:** Pain Point Priority
**Purpose:** Rank operational farm problems by severity, frequency, and effort, dividing them into Quick Wins and Long-Term Projects.
**Target Audience:** Farm managers, rural homeowners

## Key Rules & Assumptions
- **Rule 1:** All metrics are graded on a subjective scale from 1 (Lowest) to 5 (Extreme).
- **Rule 2:** Total Pain Score is an aggregate of Severity, Frequency, Cost, Time, and Risk.
- **Rule 3:** The classification logic (e.g. Quick Win vs Long-Term Project) relies on predefined thresholds for effort vs pain score.

## Step-by-Step Guide
1. **Step 1: Identify the Issue**
   - **What to do:** Name the specific problem or issue.

2. **Step 2: Score the Pain Factors**
   - **What to do:** Assign a 1 to 5 score for Severity, Frequency, Cost, Time, and Risk.
   - **Important note:** Try to be as objective as possible to keep rankings fair across issues.

3. **Step 3: Estimate Effort**
   - **What to do:** Rate the effort it would take to fix from 1 (easy) to 5 (difficult).

4. **Step 4: Review Rankings**
   - **What to expect:** The tool categorizes the issue into "Quick Win", "Long-Term Project", "Minor Fix", or "Low Priority".

## Advanced Notes / Edge Cases
- **Disclaimer:** Ratings are inherently subjective and should be used to guide conversations rather than act as definitive business directives.
