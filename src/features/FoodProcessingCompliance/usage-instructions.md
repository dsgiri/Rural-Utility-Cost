# Usage Instructions

*This file provides a standardized template for outlining how a specific calculator should be used. Include this in the calculator's dedicated feature directory if the calculator involves complex steps, heavy assumptions, or risk of misuse. Skip for trivial tools.*

## Overview
**Calculator:** Food Processing Compliance Calculator
**Purpose:** Estimate organic certification costs, label composition percentages, and assess FDA inspection readiness for agricultural processors.
**Target Audience:** Food processors, value-added farm producers, rural businesses.

## Key Rules & Assumptions
- **Rule 1:** Cost Calculator relies on standard agency baseline fee averages. Travel costs for rural areas might be higher.
- **Rule 2:** Label Percentage excludes water and salt from the total weight calculation to determine whether a product can legally use the USDA Organic seal (95%+ requirement).
- **Rule 3:** The Readiness Checklist is a baseline risk estimator and does not replace official compliance consulting or FDA guidance.

## Step-by-Step Guide
1. **Estimate Organic Cert Cost:** Use the first tab to enter your expected gross organic sales, specify if you need expedited tracking, and see your baseline fee structure.
2. **Calculate Label Percentage:** Enter all ingredients with their weights in uniform units. Mark which are organic, and critically, check "Water/Salt?" for those elements to exclude them from the formula's base weight.
3. **Review Inspection Readiness:** Fill out the checklist based on your current documentation limits. A score under 80 indicates high or moderate audit risk.

## Advanced Notes / Edge Cases
- **Disclaimer:** The FDA checklist represents generalized preventive controls compliance. You must check specific FDA requirements for high-risk categories (like dairy, meat, or acidified foods).
